Tasting Notes
Full-bodied infusion with rich cocoa undertones



Description
Steeped in ancient lore and imperial exclusivity, this legendary top Chinese tea is the finest black tea from the Fujian province. In 2011, it was served at the White House to the President of the United States and the President of China. A smooth tippy tea, rich with complex cocoa undertones, it manages to be extremely low in tannins yet rich in flavor. Originating during the Song Dynasty, this Imperial tribute tea features golden tipped black leaves consisting of one leaf and one bud.

How to Steep / Ingredients
Use 1-1.5 tsp of tea per 8oz of water. Heat water to 195-205 degrees (90-96 degrees Celsius) and steep for 2-3 minutes. For stronger flavor, use more tea leaves. 2oz of tea equals 25-30 teaspoons.

Okay, so to be honest I was really disappointed when I went into my local teavana store, because they were out of the darjeeling de triomphe black tea and I was planning on that one. I have had the capital of heaven Keemun, which I got two ounces for free of once while shopping, and was in love with it the whole time I had it until I ran out.

From the second I was transfering this tea into my tin at home, I realized I loved it. The dry smell is something like a regular black tea, but it also kind of smells a bit like roses. I didn't notice this in the store, because I was in a rush to get home. I've had myself a good share of black tea before, and was anticipating a slightly bitter cutoff or after taste at the end. For this reason, I made my first batch with three teaspoons of the tea, three teaspoons of rock sugar, and one teaspoon of the Caramel almond amaretti Herbal tea.

While it was steeping, I had the lid on, so I couldn't smell much from it, but as I was about to pour it, I opened the lid of my perfect tea maker, and BOOM... Wow. Like smelling roses made out of carmel. I had to pour some of it into two different cups because I don't have a "perfect cup." The smaller cup cooled off a bit faster so I could taste it. But after tasting it I couldn't wait for the rest to cool down and ended up using a bit of some unsweetened almond milk to help with the temperature. I then steeped this same concoction four times. After that I just had to try one batch plain.

I'm going to have to say that this tea has got to be by far the best black tea I have ever had. It's got a really dark, carmelized sugary flavor (on it's own), it is about as Smooth as butter, and has no bitter aftertaste. Doesn't really need sweetener though i always like to use it.

  • Full
  • Tea Flavor:
  • Malty, Smooth, Vegetal

Bottom Line Yes, I would recommend this to a friend

      Good, but leaves much to be desired

      By TeagerLily

      from SF, CA

      About Me Tea Enthusiast

      Pros

      • Convenient
      • Energizing
      • Good Casual Tea
      • Smooth Body

      Cons

      • Generic
      • Very Ordinary

      Best Uses

      • Morning

      Like most Teavana teas, I found this tea to be very weak with only 1 tsp and bumped it up to about 2 tsps. I brewed this between 195-200F and steeped for 3 minutes.

      The aroma is woody and damp, very similar to the smell of wet redwoods. There is a hint of sweetness to it.

      The taste is fairly full and thick while hot. However the flavor of tannins even when brewed weak is the dominant flavor. As it cools a bit, I'd be more comfortable adding that it has a woody, nutty flavor. There is a still a hint of sweetness to it, though the hints of cocoa described are nowhere to be found. Honestly, I never would have even thought it was supposed to be in there and for a minute I thought I was tasting it, but I think it was just my mind playing tricks on me, trying to distinguish cocoa somewhere in the taste.

      This tea leaves my mouth feeling both refreshed and lightly coated, the woodiness lingers, but so does the distinct taste of tannins.

      Overall, this is a pretty ordinary black tea, maybe even a bit bland in terms of the full body I was expecting from the price. Don't be fooled or persuaded by the selling notes in the description, this Golden Monkey is nothing to rave about. While I will continue to drink the rest of what I have purchased, I can certainly find better, fuller Golden Monkey elsewhere for a much more reasonable price.

      • Tea Body:
      • Medium
      • Tea Flavor:
      • Damp, Light sweetness, Slight Nuttiness, Tanins, Tannins, Thick, Woody

      Bottom Line No, I would not recommend this to a friend
