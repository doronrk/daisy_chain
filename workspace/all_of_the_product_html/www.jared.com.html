Color Stone Mother's Heart Ring

Featuring the Open Hearts by Jane Seymour design, this ring showcases the mother's round birthstone on the left side and the birthstones of her family members on the right. Engrave that special sentiment on the sides of the ring. Most natural gemstones pictured have been subject to an enhancement process or treatment (e.g. heating, oiling, diffusion, waxing, etc.), which may not be permanent and may require special care. Generally, most treated gemstone jewelry should only be gently cleaned by rinsing in warm water and dried with a soft cloth. Please refer to the FAQ section below for more information about gemstone enhancements and special care recommendations.

Estimated Arrival Date

The Estimated Arrival Date for our merchandise is displayed on product pages, in your shopping bag, and throughout the checkout process. We provide the Estimated Arrival as a guide to when your jared.com order should arrive. Jared the Galleria of Jewelry wants to be as accurate as possible when determining when you will receive your merchandise but we cannot factor certain events into the Estimated Arrival Date.

The Estimated Arrival Date DOES NOT take the following into account:

Delays in credit approval
Weather delays
Other shipping/vendor service disruptions
APO/FPO orders going through the Military Postal Service Agency (MPSA)

The Estimated Arrival Date DOES take the following into account:

Additional time needed for ring sizing
The time needed to create your personalized and special ordered items
Items shipping from different locations on different days*
Shipping method selected in checkout**

*Special orders, personalized merchandise, and Design-A-Ring orders may ship from different locations on different days, depending on the item. The Estimated Arrival Date for each item will appear in the Shopping Bag, throughout the Checkout process, and in the Order Confirmation.

**The Estimated Arrival Date on individual product pages will default to ground shipping. Selecting alternate shipping methods may change the Estimated Arrival Date.

Frequently Asked Questions

Expand All Collapse All
  • How should I care for my treated gemstone jewelry?

    Generally, most treated gemstone jewelry can be cleaned with mild liquid detergent mixed with warm water. Learn more about Color Stone Care

  • How should I care for my diamond jewelry?

    Every Jared The Galleria of Jewelry store offers complimentary cleaning and inspection for your fine jewelry. But there are a few care and cleaning suggestions that you can use to make certain that your jewelry keeps its sparkle and shine. Over time, dirt, makeup and other substances can build up on the surface of your jewelry, taking away from its natural beauty. Generally, most diamond jewelry can be cleaned with mild liquid detergent mixed with warm water.

  • Do all rings come in the same size or are there different sizes available?

    All of our rings are sized to what has been determined is an average finger size. When you purchase a ring on jared.com, you will be shown the size (or sizes) in which the ring is available. If your finger is a different size, we can adjust the ring. If you are unsure about your ring size, please visit JewelryWise.com . Our Ring Sizer tool will help you figure out what size you need.

  • Do you offer engraving?

    Engraving is available for most fine jewelry purchases at any Jared location. On jared.com, engraving is available only for personalized items, where indicated.

  • How should I care for the metal in my ring?

    When it comes to caring for your metal, it's important to remember that every metal is different. While little maintenance is needed for durable metals such as tungsten, other metals require some attention. For instance, platinum is a strong metal but is susceptible to scratches. Gold is another metal that needs gentle care. Soap film easily builds on the surface of gold, so it's best to remove your gold jewelry before showering or using household cleaners or chemicals. Are you looking for more detailed information? Click here to learn more at JewelryWise.com

  • How should I care for my cultured pearls?

    When getting dressed, cultured pearls should be the last item put on and the first item taken off. Makeup, hairspray, perfume, and other chemicals are very harmful to cultured pearls. They can be gently washed in a mild soap or wiped with a damp cloth to remove daily build up and dirt. They should also be separated from other jewelry to avoid nicks and scratches. Click here to learn more about Pearls and how to care for them

  • Why do you measure jewelry in millimeters?

    The millimeter is our standard unit of measurement for jewelry dimensions. By using millimeters, we can provide you with the most accurate height, width and length for your jewelry. To give you a better idea of your jewelry's dimensions, 25 mm is approximately 1 inch. See below for other approximate conversions.

    3 mm is approximately 1/8 inch

    6 mm is approximately 1/4 inch

    13 mm is approximately 1/2 inch

    19 mm is approximately 3/4 inch

    25 mm is approximately 1 inch

    50 mm is approximately 2 inches

    75 mm is approximately 3 inches

  • What is the difference between white gold and platinum?

    White gold is pure gold that is mixed with alloys to give it a white color. Platinum is pure metal that is naturally white and much more durable and stronger than white gold.

    Are you looking for more detailed information? Click here to learn more at JewelryWise.com

  • What is the difference between carat and karat?

    A carat is a unit of measure for diamond weight and is evaluated on a point system. One carat is equivalent to 100 points.

    A karat is the standard measurement of gold, which is divided into 24 parts. Pure gold is 24 karats, meaning 24 out of 24 parts are gold.

  • What are contemporary metals?

    Metals such as titanium, stainless steel and tungsten are considered non-traditional jewelry metals. These metals are growing in popularity and offer variety when shopping for jewelry.

    Are you looking for more detailed information? Click here to learn more at JewelryWise.com

  • What determines the price of a diamond?

    A diamond's value is determined by its carat weight, color, cut and clarity. To get a better understanding of the 4Cs and see how each C affects the price of a diamond, please visit our Diamond Guide.

  • What are the 4Cs of diamonds?

    The 4Cs of diamonds are the characteristics that determine the value of a diamond. They are: Cut, Clarity, Color, Carat. For more information about the 4Cs and how to prioritize them please visit JewelryWise.com , your expert source for jewelry advice.
