Slinger 72-in Brushed Nickel Downrod Mount Ceiling Fan with Light Kit and Remote Control ENERGY STAR

  • Contemporary style 72-in diameter, Indoor only brushed nickel ceiling fan with integrated frosted glass light kit
  • Remote control features include: 6 speed options, light control button and seasonal reverse option
  • Nine cocoa finish blades
  • Includes 32W CFL GU24 light bulb, ¾-in diameter x 6-in long down rod and 54-in lead wire Includes 100W halogen E11 light bulb, ¾” diameter x 4.5” long down rod and 54” lead wire
  • Height from ceiling to the bottom of the fan is 21.13-in with a 14° blade pitch
  • 6 speed energy efficient DC motor
  • Down rod mount only; flat ceiling or sloped ceiling with 19° max slope
  • Limited lifetime motor warranty, 2 year warranty on all other parts
  • Energy star qualified

I bought this fan with some hesitation because of complaints about the unusual light bulb and difficulty in getting the fan to work out of the box. Unfortunately, Lowes doesn't have an on-line manual for the fan, so many questions awaited the box opening.
As to the light fixture . . . it is a GU24 base, which is strange. High wattage GU24 CFLs and LEDs are not readily available in stores. The fan comes with a warm non-dimmable 32 watt CFL, equivalent to a 125 watt incandescent bulb.
The instructions are unclear with respect to use of dimmable CFL bulbs. They state that the dimmer can be used with any type bulb except CFL, "which cannot be dimmed." What is not clear is whether the dimmer will work with a dimmable CFLS or not. The way it is written the manufacturer seems to be saying the CFL bulbs themselves cannot be dimmed. Of course, that used to be true, but is no longer. We'll see, if I try it.
The instructions may be a bit old, because high wattage CFL dimmable bulbs are available on the web, but NOT with a GU24 base. That is easily fixed. Simply replace the GU24 receptacle on the fixture with an E26 recptacle (medium screw-in), available at Lowes and practically everywhere. There is also a GU24 to E26 adapter available at Amazon for less than $2. That said, using an adapter could be a problem because it adds about 1 1/4" to the receptacle which could limit you to bulbs that are short. The length from the edge of the supplied receptacle to the bottom of the glass globe is 7". That means you are limited to bulbs with a Maximum Overall Length (MOL) of about 5.9" if you use an adapter.
Most available 100 to 125 watt equivalent dimmable CFLs and LEDs have the E26 base. Feit offers a 100 watt equivalent dimmable LED, model A/OM1600R/LED, which is 5.2" Maximum Overall Length (MOL), and will cost you about $38. That will clearly fit even with the adapter. Phillips offers the 407155 - 32 Watt - CFL, which is 5.98" MOL and dimmable. One might have to replace the GU24 receptacle with an E26 receptacle to fit this bulb inside the globe. The Phillips bulb is slightly less expensive at $29.15. I've ordered both to see if they will fit with an adapter.
Putting the fan together was a snap, with a phillips screw driver . . . less than an hour, except for the blades which can't be put in place until the fan is mounted. It took my electrician less than an hour to mount the fan on a 14' dome, install the blades, and synchronize the fan.
Though others may have had trouble with the fan starting, this one started to synch immediately when powered on. In less than 5 minutes it was finished. No wobble noted anywhere. It was rock solid and totally silent, and required no balancing of the blades. The supplied 32 watt CFL looks nice as well. I haven't put in a dimmable bulb yet (waiting for the adapter from Amazon). But will try it when the bulbs and adapter arrive.
The fan has a stunningly different look than almost anything out there. Contrary to manufacturer instructions, I mounted the fan in a covered courtyard. It will be subject to high temperatures, and some humidity there, but will be dry. I'm not concerned about rust of the finish. If that happens over the years, I'll just replace the fan. The finish on the product was excellent, and there were plenty of screws, in case one got lost down a drain pipe during installation. No rough edges on the machined parts. Everything worked right out of the box with no stumbling. All in all an easy installation and a remarkable result.
One note to those who purchase the fan. There are multiple warnings in the box about the start-up of this fan. It MUST be synchronized with the remote control within 60 seconds of power on. You have to follow the instructions to get it to work.
Bottom line? If you buy a $300 fan, you shouldn't be worrying about the $30 cost of a dimmable bulb. Whether a 32 watt CFL can actually be dimmed with this fan is still an unknown, but it looks terrific with the supplied bulb in my own application.
Pros High quality, plastic blades
I would recommend this to a friend

Fan is on display in my local Lowe's store, but must be ordered. Finished product functioned exactly as described with no vibration. Unit is quiet & moves a lot of air. With the remote control only one electrical line is needed as it also controls the light. We had the builder wire for two lines so now I have an extra switch. When turned off with the wall switch the unit comes on as it was previously set with the remote. This is a good feature as much of the time I just want the light to come on so the one wall switch acts like a light on and off. The 32 Watt CFL Bulb is equivalent to a tungsten bulb of 120 Watts. Color is 2700K which makes for a pleasant & bright 2200 Lumens of light.
