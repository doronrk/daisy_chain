Starbucks Reserve® Pour-Over Cone

One of the best ways to enjoy extraordinary Starbucks® Reserve coffees, this pour-over manual brewing system regulates the flow to bring out the full-bodied, aromatic flavors from your favorite coffee—and it's so easy to use. Just place the ceramic cone with a #4 paper filter over a mug and brew. Dishwasher safe.

  • Place the ceramic cone on a mug
  • Insert a #4 paper cone filter and scoop in 4 TBSP coarsely ground coffee
  • Pour in 12 fl oz of hot water
  • Enjoy freshly brewed coffee by the cup!
  • Ceramic pour-over cone is dishwasher safe.
  • Pour-over system uses Starbucks or Melitta filters.

Drip hole too big
I really wanted to have a ceramic pour-over cone, as I hate the idea of my coffee running past a plastic cone on its way into my cup... plastic chemicals leaching into my coffee? No thanks.
But I'm disappointed with this particular one: the water runs through it too quickly, and consequently, my coffee isn't strong enough. I've been tearing off two layers of paper filter to add over the hole at the bottom, underneath my regular filter, to try to slow the water flow down some. I'm disappointed.
I don't want to spend the money to ship it back, and I don't have extra money in my life to just buy something else.
No, I do not recommend this product.

Great Product with one issue...
Bought this to complete my setup. The unit is great and of very good quality. I enjoy the Reserve logo as subtle art.
I had previously purchased the Wood Pour-Over Stand and #4 Cone Set which comes with a #4 Starbucks porcelin all white pour over unit. This purchase was to complete the setup that can hold 2 units.
However the cone that came with the Wood set has a deep rim diameter (2 inch) on bottom so it fits in the precut hole in the stand.
This Reserve unit has a very short but larger diameter rim (2.75 inch) than the precut hole. So while you can line it up so it flows well, there is nothing to prevent it from sliding around side to side while sitting on the top of the stand.
Yes, I recommend this product.

This makes a superb cup of coffee. But besides loving a great cup of coffee, I'm not sure I understand if all of the different kinds of coffee makers are worth cleaning -- when a person can learn to master just the plain 'ol drip maker (especially with an automatic timer}. I have used a couple of different French Presses, a couple of different Italian coffee makers, and now this "pour over brew." Again, they all work well if you just want 1 or 2 cups of great tasting coffee. But again, I don't know if the coffee's ENOUGH BETTER to warrant cleaning all of these gadgets.
